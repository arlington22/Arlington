CATHOLIC SCHOOLS

The Catholic schools exist primarily for the purpose of sharing with students the principles of Christian Education which according to the Second Vatican Council: "...aim at the foundation of human persons with respect to their ultimate goal, and simultaneously with respect to the good of those socities of which as persons they are members and in whose responbilities, as adults they share.

THE ADMINISTRATION

The administration of an instution/school comprises an Administrative Team that consists of the Principal, the Vice Principal(s), the Dean, the Registrar,the Guidance Counselor and the Business Manager with the pasto, in his absence, the Parish Catechist, serving as Ex-Officio/Advisor. The Team is chaired by the Principal, or the Vice Principal for administration in the Principal's absence, and cites members to meetings per schedule or as need be. Though the Principal is the highest authority within the instutuion/school, this team serves to broaden the administrative decision-making process.

THE STUDENTS

The student in a catholic instutionk is there for a purpose: to be prepared to live with dignity presently and in the future, with an awareness of God and of his/her fianl destiny. Coopreration with and love and respect for authority, for peers, for parents/guardians and thereby give meaning to existence as a creature in the likeness of the Creator.

EXTRA-CURRICULA ACTIVITIES

The school exists to help the student develop not only intellectually, but also spiritually, socially, physically, morally and psychologically. Hence, the need for extra-curricula activities does not and must not take precedence over academics. These activities are not limited to sports, but also include drama, dancing, singing, debates, etc. Special care should be taken to help students develop their talents fruitfully in whatever direction. Extra-curricula activities are also to be used to bring the schools of the Diocese together in a spirit of wholesome competition so as to foster not only excellent sportmanship, but also the sense of belonging to one caring family.
